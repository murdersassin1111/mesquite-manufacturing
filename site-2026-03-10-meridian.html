FAQ

Common Questions Answered

Everything engineers, buyers, and project managers need to know before requesting a quote.

What CNC machining tolerances can you achieve?

We hold tolerances from ±0.001″ to ±0.005″ across our precision CNC machining, milling, and 3D printing services. Share your drawing and we'll confirm achievability on your specific geometry.

What is fourth axis CNC machining?

4th axis CNC adds rotation to the standard 3-axis (X/Y/Z) movement, enabling intricate geometries and undercut features in a single setup. It allows high-volume production of complex parts at lower cost than competitors using only 3-axis machines.

What materials do you machine?

We work with a broad range including metals (aluminum, steel, titanium, brass), plastics (Delrin, PEEK, nylon, ABS), and composites. Not sure what material fits your application? We're happy to advise.

Can you handle both prototypes and production runs?

Absolutely. Our Prototype Development service is built for rapid iteration, while our 4th axis CNC systems scale to high-volume production efficiently. We support parts at every stage of your product lifecycle.

Do you offer CNC milling?

Yes — precision CNC milling is a core service. We combine milling with turning and 4th axis capability to machine complex parts to exacting specifications. CNC milling is included under our Precision Machining service.

Do you offer 3D printing?

Yes. Our additive manufacturing service delivers ±0.001″–0.005″ tolerances in traditional plastics, innovative composites, and metals — ideal for complex geometries, functional prototypes, and specialty low-volume parts.

How do I get a quote?

Fill out the RFQ form on this page — we respond within 24 hours, 7 days a week. Include your part description, material, quantity, and tolerance requirements. Attach a drawing or CAD file for the most accurate quote.

How fast can you turn around a project?

We operate 24/7 to supply the best service possible. Lead times vary by complexity, quantity, and material — contact us via the RFQ form and we'll provide a timeline specific to your project requirements.
