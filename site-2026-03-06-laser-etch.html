FAQ

Common Questions

Everything engineers and buyers need to know before submitting an RFQ.

What CNC machining tolerances can you achieve?

We achieve tolerances of ±0.001″ to ±0.005″ on our CNC machining and milling operations. Our 4th Axis capability allows us to hold tight tolerances on complex geometries that require simultaneous multi-axis movement.

What is fourth axis CNC machining?

Fourth axis CNC adds rotational movement to traditional 3-axis machining, enabling intricate geometries at high volume production rates. This allows Mesquite Manufacturing to produce parts at lower cost than competitors while maintaining exceptional precision.

What materials do you machine?

We machine a wide range of materials including metals (aluminum, steel, stainless, titanium, brass, copper), plastics (Delrin, UHMW, nylon, PEEK, polycarbonate), and composites. Contact us about your specific material requirements.

Can you handle both prototypes and production runs?

Absolutely. We offer both prototype development and full production runs. Our rapid prototyping service helps you validate designs quickly, and our 4th Axis CNC technology scales efficiently to high-volume production at competitive cost.

Do you offer CNC milling?

Yes — precision CNC milling is a core service at Mesquite Manufacturing. We provide multi-axis milling for complex profiles, pockets, slots, and surface finishing operations on metals, plastics, and composites.

Do you offer 3D printing?

Yes. Our additive manufacturing services produce parts in traditional plastics, innovative composites, and metals — achieving tolerances of ±0.001″–±0.005″. 3D printing complements our CNC machining for complex geometries and rapid prototyping needs.

How do I get a quote?

Fill out the RFQ form on this page with your project details, material, quantity, and tolerance requirements. We respond within 24 hours, 7 days a week — including weekends. The quote is always free with no obligation.

How fast can you turn around a project?

We work 24/7 to supply the best service possible. Lead time varies by project complexity, material availability, and quantity. Submit your RFQ with your timeline requirements and we'll provide a specific turnaround estimate with your quote.
