KNOWLEDGE BASE

Frequently Asked
Technical Questions

Engineering teams ask. We answer. Everything you need to know before you quote.

What CNC machining tolerances can you achieve?

We machine to ±0.001″ – ±0.005″ tolerances across our precision CNC machining, milling, and 3D printing services. For project-specific tolerance requirements, submit your RFQ and we'll confirm achievability.

What is fourth axis (4th axis) CNC machining?

4th axis CNC adds a rotational axis to standard 3-axis machining, enabling intricate geometries at high production volume. Our 4th axis solutions manufacture complex parts at lower cost than competitors — it's our core differentiator.

What materials do you machine?

We work with metals, plastics, and composites. From aluminum and steel to engineering plastics to advanced composite materials — across CNC machining, milling, and 3D printing. Contact us for specific alloy or material questions.

Can you handle both prototypes and production runs?

Yes — both are core services. Our Prototype Development service handles rapid prototyping with iterative feedback, while our Precision Machining handles high-volume production using 4th axis CNC to keep costs competitive.

Do you offer CNC milling?

Yes. CNC milling is a core service. Our precision CNC machining and milling capabilities include 4th axis technology for complex parts, materials including metals, plastics, and composites, all to ±0.001″–0.005″ tolerances.

Do you offer 3D printing?

Yes. We offer additive manufacturing (3D printing) to ±0.001″–0.005″ tolerances in traditional plastics, innovative composites, and metals. Ideal for complex geometries, prototypes, fixtures, and end-use parts.

How do I get a quote?

Fill out the RFQ form on this page — we respond within 24 hours, 7 days a week. Include your part description, material, quantity, and tolerance requirements. Upload a drawing or CAD file if available for a faster, more accurate quote.

How fast can you turn around a project?

We operate 24/7 to supply the best service possible. Lead times vary by complexity, quantity, and material. Submit your RFQ with your required timeline and we'll confirm availability and schedule during quoting.
