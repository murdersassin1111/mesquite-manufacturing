FAQ

Common Questions Answered

Everything engineers and buyers want to know before placing an order.

What CNC machining tolerances can you achieve?
We achieve tolerances of ±0.001″ to ±0.005″ on our CNC machining and milling work, as well as our 3D printing services. For critical-tolerance applications, share your drawing and we'll confirm feasibility in your quote.
What is fourth axis (4th axis) CNC machining?
Fourth axis CNC adds a rotational axis (A-axis) to standard 3-axis machining. This enables intricate geometries — undercuts, helical features, and complex contours — in a single setup, at high volume, and at lower cost than conventional methods. It's a key differentiator we're proud of.
What materials do you machine?
We machine metals (aluminum, steel, stainless, titanium, and more), engineering plastics (PEEK, Delrin, nylon, etc.), and composites. If you have a specific material in mind, include it in your RFQ and we'll advise on machinability.
Can you handle both prototypes and production runs?
Absolutely. We offer full prototype development services for design validation, and we scale to production volumes. Many customers prototype with us and continue to full production runs — no need to re-qualify a new supplier.
Do you offer CNC milling?
Yes — precision CNC milling is a core service. We use multi-axis milling to produce flat surfaces, pockets, slots, and complex contoured features across metals, plastics, and composites.
Do you offer 3D printing?
Yes. Our additive manufacturing service achieves ±0.001″–0.005″ tolerances and works with traditional plastics, innovative composites, and metal materials. It's ideal for complex geometries or rapid design iteration.
How do I get a quote?
Fill out the RFQ form on this page — include your part description, quantity, material, tolerance requirements, and any drawings or CAD files. We respond within 24 hours, 7 days a week, with a detailed, customized quote.
How fast can you turn around a project?
We work 24/7 to supply the best service possible. Lead times vary by project complexity, quantity, and material. Submit your RFQ with your timeline and we'll do everything we can to meet it — or advise on expedited options.
