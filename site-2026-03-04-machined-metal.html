Frequently Asked Questions

Everything engineers and procurement managers want to know before submitting an RFQ.

What CNC machining tolerances can you achieve?

We routinely achieve ±0.001″ to ±0.005″ across our CNC machining and 3D printing operations. For project-specific tolerance requirements, include them in your RFQ and we'll confirm feasibility.

What is fourth axis CNC machining?

4th axis CNC adds rotational movement (A-axis) to standard 3-axis milling, enabling complex geometries and undercut features without repositioning. This allows us to manufacture high-volume parts with intricate designs at lower cost than competitors.

What materials do you machine?

We work with metals (aluminum, steel, titanium, brass, and more), plastics, and composites. Have a specific material? Include it in your RFQ and our team will confirm compatibility.

Can you handle both prototypes and production runs?

Yes. We offer dedicated prototype development for rapid validation and refinement, as well as high-volume production runs using our 4th axis CNC technology. Many customers start with a prototype and scale to production with us.

Do you offer CNC milling?

Yes — precision CNC milling is a core capability. Our multi-axis milling centers handle everything from simple 2D profiles to complex 3D contoured surfaces and 4th-axis rotational features.

Do you offer 3D printing?

Yes. Our additive manufacturing operation produces parts to ±0.001″–0.005″ tolerances in traditional plastics, innovative composites, and metals — the same precision standard as our CNC machining services.

How do I get a quote?

Fill out the RFQ form on this page with your project details. We respond within 24 hours, 7 days a week — free quote, no obligation. The more detail you provide (material, tolerance, quantity, timeline), the faster we can respond.

How fast can you turn around a project?

We operate 24/7 to supply the best service possible. Lead time varies by project complexity, quantity, and material availability. Contact us with your specific requirements and we'll give you an accurate timeline in your quote.
