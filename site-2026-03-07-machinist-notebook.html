FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything engineers and buyers ask before placing their first order.

Q.01

What CNC machining tolerances can you achieve?

We hold tolerances of ±0.001″ to ±0.005″ depending on the feature, material, and geometry. Our 4th axis CNC allows us to maintain tight tolerances on complex, multi-face parts that standard 3-axis machining cannot access in one setup.

Q.02

What is fourth axis CNC machining?

Fourth axis (4th axis) CNC machining adds rotational movement (A-axis) to traditional X/Y/Z machining. This enables intricate geometries at high volume, reducing setups and lowering per-part cost significantly compared to 3-axis competitors.

Q.03

What materials do you machine?

We work with metals, plastics, and composites — including aluminum, steel, stainless, titanium, ABS, PEEK, Delrin, nylon, carbon fiber composites, and more. Contact us with your specific material and we'll confirm capability.

Q.04

Can you handle both prototypes and production runs?

Yes — both. Our Prototype Development service handles rapid first articles and design validation, while our Precision Machining capability handles full production volumes. We scale with your project.

Q.05

Do you offer CNC milling?

Yes. Precision CNC milling is a core service. We offer 3-axis and 4th axis CNC milling with tolerances from ±0.001″, across metals, plastics, and composites. Our milling capability is integrated with our quality assurance process.

Q.06

Do you offer 3D printing?

Yes. Our additive manufacturing service achieves tolerances of ±0.001″–0.005″ in traditional plastics, innovative composites, and metals. 3D printing is ideal for prototyping, low-volume parts, and complex geometries.

Q.07

How do I get a quote?

Fill out the RFQ form on this page — describe your part, upload a drawing if available, and select your service. We respond within 24 hours, 7 days a week. No obligation, always free.

Q.08

How fast can you turn around a project?

We are available 24/7 and work to supply the best service possible. Lead time depends on your specific project — material, complexity, quantity, and finish. Contact us with your details for an accurate timeline.
