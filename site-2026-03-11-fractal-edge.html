05 — KNOWLEDGE BASE

Frequently Asked
Questions

Everything engineers and procurement teams ask before submitting their first RFQ.

What CNC machining tolerances can you achieve?

We achieve tolerances of ±0.001″ to ±0.005″ depending on the part geometry, material, and process. Our 4th axis CNC capability allows us to maintain precision across complex multi-surface parts that would be difficult with conventional 3-axis machining.

What is fourth axis CNC machining?

Fourth (4th) axis CNC adds rotational movement to standard 3-axis machining, enabling intricate geometries to be machined without repositioning the part. This allows us to manufacture high-volume complex parts at significantly lower cost than competitors who rely on multiple setups.

What materials do you machine?

We work with a full range of metals (steel, aluminum, titanium, stainless, brass, copper), plastics (PEEK, Delrin, nylon, polycarbonate, ABS), and composites. If your project requires a specific material, contact us to confirm availability.

Can you handle both prototypes and production runs?

Yes — we offer both prototype development for design validation and full production runs. Our 4th axis CNC capability is particularly valuable for production, enabling high-volume output at lower per-part cost. Many customers start with a prototype and scale directly to production with us.

Do you offer CNC milling?

Yes. Precision CNC milling is a core service at Mesquite Manufacturing. We perform both conventional and 4th axis milling to ±0.001″ tolerances across metals, plastics, and composites.

Do you offer 3D printing?

Yes. Our additive manufacturing capability produces parts in traditional plastics, innovative composites, and metals to ±0.001″–0.005″ tolerances. 3D printing is often used alongside CNC machining for complete part solutions and rapid prototyping.

How do I get a quote?

Fill out the RFQ form on this page — describe your part, specify material and quantity, and optionally upload your drawing or CAD file. We respond within 24 hours, 7 days a week. You can also use the quick form above the fold for an even faster first contact.

How fast can you turn around a project?

We work 24/7 to supply the best service possible. Lead time depends on part complexity, quantity, and material availability. Contact us with your specific project details and we'll provide a realistic timeline with your quote.
