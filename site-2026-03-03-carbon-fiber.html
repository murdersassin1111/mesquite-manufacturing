Questions & Answers

Frequently Asked
Questions

What CNC machining tolerances can you achieve?

We routinely achieve ±0.001″ to ±0.005″ tolerances on our CNC machined parts, including milled components. Our 4th axis CNC technology and quality control processes ensure these tolerances are maintained across the entire run.

What is fourth axis CNC machining?

Fourth axis CNC adds a rotational axis (A-axis) to standard 3-axis machining. This enables intricate geometries, undercuts, and complex contours that would otherwise require multiple setups — resulting in higher quality at higher volume, at lower cost than our competitors.

What materials do you machine?

We machine metals (steel, aluminum, titanium, brass, and more), plastics (HDPE, Delrin, PEEK, nylon, and others), and composites including fiber-reinforced materials. Our 3D printing also supports innovative composites and metal powders.

Can you handle both prototypes and production runs?

Absolutely. We offer dedicated Prototype Development services for concept validation and design refinement, and full-scale Precision Machining for high-volume production. Many customers prototype with us and then move directly into production.

Do you offer CNC milling?

Yes — precision CNC milling is a core service at Mesquite Manufacturing. Combined with our 4th axis capability, we can mill complex parts that traditional 3-axis mills cannot produce in a single setup.

Do you offer 3D printing?

Yes. Our additive manufacturing service achieves ±0.001″–0.005″ tolerances using traditional plastics, innovative composites, and metals — making it a serious production option, not just a prototyping tool.

How do I get a quote?

Fill out the RFQ form on this page — include your part description, material, quantity, and tolerance requirements. We respond within 24 hours, 7 days a week. It's free and there's no obligation.

How fast can you turn around a project?

We work 24/7 to supply the best service possible. Turnaround depends on part complexity, quantity, and material. Submit your RFQ and we'll provide a specific lead time with your quote — typically within 24 hours of your request.
