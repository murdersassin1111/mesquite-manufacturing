FAQ

Precision Answers

Common questions from engineers, buyers, and production managers.

Q_01:// What CNC machining tolerances can you achieve?
We routinely achieve tolerances of ±0.001″ to ±0.005″ across CNC machining, CNC milling, and 3D printing. Our 4th axis CNC capability allows tight tolerances on complex multi-face geometries.
Q_02:// What is fourth axis CNC machining?
Fourth (4th) axis CNC adds a rotational axis (A-axis) to standard 3-axis machining. This enables intricate geometries on curved and cylindrical surfaces in a single setup — delivering higher volume production at lower cost than multi-setup alternatives.
Q_03:// What materials do you machine?
We work with a wide range of materials including metals (aluminum, steel, stainless, titanium, brass, copper), plastics (ABS, Delrin, PEEK, nylon, UHMW), and composites. Contact us if you have a specific material requirement.
Q_04:// Can you handle both prototypes and production runs?
Yes — both prototype development and full production runs are core offerings. We support the entire lifecycle: rapid prototyping for design validation, then scaling to production volume with our 4th axis CNC technology.
Q_05:// Do you offer CNC milling?
Yes — precision CNC milling is a core service. We provide high-accuracy milling on complex geometries with our advanced CNC equipment, achieving ±0.001″ tolerances across a broad range of materials.
Q_06:// Do you offer 3D printing?
Yes — our additive manufacturing service produces parts to ±0.001″–0.005″ tolerances using traditional plastics, innovative composites, and metals. 3D printing is often ideal for complex geometries and rapid prototyping.
Q_07:// How do I get a quote?
Fill out the RFQ form on this page — describe your part, quantity, tolerances, material, and timeline. Optionally attach a drawing or CAD file. We respond within 24 hours, 7 days a week, with no obligation.
Q_08:// How fast can you turn around a project?
We're available 24/7 and work continuously to supply the best service possible. Lead time depends on part complexity, material, and volume — contact us with your project details for a specific timeline estimate.
