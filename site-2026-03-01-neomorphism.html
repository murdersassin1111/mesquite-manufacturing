Common Questions

Everything You Need to Know

Answers to the questions engineers and procurement teams ask us most.

What CNC machining tolerances can you achieve?
We consistently achieve ±0.001″ to ±0.005″ across CNC machining, CNC milling, and 3D printing operations. Our 4th axis capability allows us to maintain these tolerances on complex geometry parts as well.
What is fourth axis CNC machining?
Fourth axis (4th axis) CNC machining adds a rotational axis (A-axis) to standard 3-axis machining. This enables the manufacture of intricate, multi-sided geometries in fewer setups — resulting in higher consistency and lower cost at volume versus traditional approaches.
What materials do you machine?
We work across metals (aluminum, steel, stainless, brass, titanium, and more), engineering plastics (PEEK, Delrin, nylon, HDPE, and more), and advanced composites. Not sure about your material? Ask in your RFQ and we'll advise.
Can you handle both prototypes and production runs?
Absolutely. We offer dedicated Prototype Development services for rapid design validation, as well as full production CNC machining using our 4th axis technology for high-volume runs at competitive cost.
Do you offer CNC milling?
Yes — precision CNC milling is a core service at Mesquite Manufacturing. Our milling capabilities include multi-axis operations and are available across all material types we support.
Do you offer 3D printing?
Yes. Our additive manufacturing service produces functional parts in traditional plastics, innovative composites, and metals — all to ±0.001″–0.005″ tolerances, suitable for both prototypes and end-use components.
How do I get a quote?
Fill out the RFQ form on this page with your project details and we'll respond within 24 hours, 7 days a week. You can also attach a drawing or CAD file to help us provide the most accurate quote possible.
How fast can you turn around a project?
We operate 24/7 to supply the best service possible. Lead times vary based on complexity, material availability, and quantity — contact us with your specific project for a lead time estimate when you submit your RFQ.
