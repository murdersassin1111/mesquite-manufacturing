FAQ

Common Questions
from Engineers & Buyers

Everything you need to know before requesting your quote.

What CNC machining tolerances can you achieve?

We hold tolerances of ±0.001″ to ±0.005″ depending on material, geometry, and process. Our 4th axis CNC capability maintains these tolerances across complex multi-face features.

What is fourth axis (4th axis) CNC machining?

4th axis CNC adds a rotational axis to standard 3-axis machining, enabling intricate geometries and undercuts in a single setup. The result: higher precision, less handling, and lower cost per part at volume.

What materials do you machine?

We work with metals (aluminum, steel, stainless, titanium), engineering plastics (Delrin, PEEK, nylon), and composites. Not sure what material you need? We'll advise based on your application.

Can you handle both prototypes and production runs?

Absolutely. We offer rapid prototype development for design validation and full production-volume machining once you're ready to scale. The same team handles both — ensuring consistency from first prototype to final part.

Do you offer CNC milling?

Yes — precision CNC milling is a core service at Mesquite Manufacturing. We perform flat-surface milling, contour milling, pocket milling, and complex profile work using our advanced CNC milling centers.

Do you offer 3D printing / additive manufacturing?

Yes. Our industrial 3D printing service achieves ±0.001″–0.005″ tolerances and supports traditional plastics, innovative composites, and metals — ideal for complex geometries and low-to-medium volume parts.

How do I get a quote?

Fill out the RFQ form on this page — include your part description, material, quantity, and tolerance requirements. We respond within 24 hours, 7 days a week. You can also attach a drawing or CAD file for a more precise quote.

How fast can you turn around a project?

We're available 24/7 and work hard to meet your timeline. Lead time depends on part complexity, material, and quantity. Contact us with your deadline and we'll be upfront about what's achievable.
