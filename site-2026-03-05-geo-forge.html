Frequently Asked

Engineer FAQ

Answers to the questions engineers ask most before submitting an RFQ.

What CNC machining tolerances can you achieve?

We routinely achieve ±0.001″ to ±0.005″ tolerances on both CNC machining/milling and 3D printing operations. Our 4th axis CNC technology enables exceptional precision on complex geometries.

What is fourth axis CNC machining?

4th axis CNC adds rotational movement to the standard 3-axis setup, enabling intricate multi-face geometries in a single setup. This allows us to manufacture high-volume complex parts at significantly lower cost than conventional multi-setup machining.

What materials do you machine?

We work with metals, plastics, and composites. Whether you need aluminum, steel, titanium, engineering plastics, or advanced composites — we have the tooling and expertise to machine it precisely.

Can you handle both prototypes and production runs?

Absolutely. Prototype development and production machining are both core services. We help you validate your design through rapid prototyping, then scale to high-volume production — all in one place.

Do you offer CNC milling?

Yes — precision CNC milling is one of our core services. Our 4th axis milling capability handles complex contours, pockets, and surface profiles that standard 3-axis mills cannot achieve in a single setup.

Do you offer 3D printing?

Yes. We offer additive manufacturing with ±0.001″–0.005″ tolerances using traditional plastics, innovative composites, and metal materials. Ideal for complex geometries, rapid iteration, and low-volume production.

How do I get a quote?

Fill out the RFQ form on this page — describe your part, select service and material, optionally attach a drawing or CAD file. We respond within 24 hours, 7 days a week with a tailored quote. No obligation.

How fast can you turn around a project?

We work 24/7 to supply the best service possible. Lead time depends on your specific project's complexity, quantity, and material. Contact us via the RFQ form for a precise lead time estimate for your job.
